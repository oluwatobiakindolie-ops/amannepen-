Self-Awakening

One sign that you're growing in life is when you begin to question everything around you. Whether consciously or unconsciously, in many African homes, an inquisitive child is often silenced. Curious children are considered talkative, hyperactive children as troublesome, and enthusiastic children as being too forward.

If those qualities weren't constantly suppressed, many children would grow up less afraid to challenge ideas and less likely to conform blindly to society's expectations. After al , many of the norms we follow are shaped by the social and cultural environments we grow up in. The truth about norms is that they are unwritten rules that determine which behaviours, values, and traditions are considered acceptable. From an early age, you are told to keep quiet. You are discouraged from asking difficult questions or looking beyond the obvious. Over time, you begin to accept what you are told as absolute truth, and that mindset can influence every area of your life. But the day you start questioning what you have always accepted as truth is the day your self-awakening begins. It is as though your mind starts returning to its original state—curious, observant, eager to learn, and unafraid to ask why.

The child who once wanted to understand everything around them begins to re-emerge. As a young person, it's okay not to conform to your environment when there are still questions waiting to be answered. Growth begins the moment you stop accepting everything at face value and start seeking understanding for yourself.
